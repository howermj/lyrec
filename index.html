Getting Started

Scanning

Tap Start Scanner and point your camera at the barcode on the back of a CD, vinyl, or cassette. lyrec checks your library and tells you whether to buy it.

You can also type a barcode number manually or search by artist and album name.

Recommendations

Buy Album is incomplete -- buy to fill gaps
Consider Unknown completion or mixed format
Upgrade Complete but low-quality lossy -- upgrade when convenient
Wait Complete, high-quality lossy -- no rush
Skip Already lossless and complete -- you're good

Data freshness

The status bar shows library coverage and when your data was last updated. Data refreshes automatically when you open the app with a network connection.

History

Every scan is saved for 24 hours. Open the menu and tap History to review what you scanned today, grouped by recommendation. Tap any item to see the full result again.

Dismissing results

Tap "Scan Next", tap outside the card, or swipe down to dismiss a result and return to scanning.

Splash image

Tap the image on the main screen to cycle through Lyra's artwork.

Scanner stuck?

If the scanner hasn't detected a barcode after several seconds, tap the circular arrow button on the camera to restart it. The app will also auto-restart the scanner if it stalls for more than 20 seconds.

What it does

lyrec is a field companion for music collectors. Scan a UPC barcode on a CD, vinyl, or cassette at a record store and instantly know whether it's worth buying based on what's already in your library.

Technology

lyrec is a Progressive Web App. Barcode scanning uses the ZXing WASM engine via the barcode-detector polyfill. All data is cached locally -- the app works fully offline at the store.

Library data is generated nightly by R70 (part of LyraTools) from Phase A sidecar metadata and pushed to GitHub Pages.
